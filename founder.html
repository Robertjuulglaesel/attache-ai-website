I've hired assistants in Argentina. In the Philippines. It never worked.

Not because they weren't good. Because by the time someone knows you well enough to actually help you — they leave. Or double their rate. And you start over.

I read The 4-Hour Work Week and wanted that life. I just couldn't hold onto it.

Then AI arrived. And I saw it immediately: this is the assistant that doesn't quit.

But I wasn't going to rent it from Google.

The moment something becomes truly valuable, you don't want a landlord. A landlord raises your rent. A landlord can lock you out. A landlord owns the thing — not you.

So I built Attaché AI.

Your assistant runs on your hardware. Your Mac Mini. Your data never leaves. The context you build over months and years — every preference, every habit, every shortcut — is yours to keep. Cancel tomorrow and walk away with everything.

No one can price you out when it becomes indispensable. No one can take it away.

We help you build it. We help you run it. You train it. You own it.

Forever.
